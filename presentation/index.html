The idea
In one sentence

Every handshake at UKREiiF turns into a captured lead — without anyone fishing for a business card.

"Nice to meet you — scan this and we'll send you the pack."

That single line is the whole interaction. The lanyard does the work, the lander does the form, the pipeline does the rest.

The not-obvious bits
Where this beats a paper card

Four things the lanyard knows that a business card never could.

01 · Attribution

Who introduced them

Each lanyard's QR carries a delegate code. Every lead is tagged with whoever was wearing the badge they scanned.

?d=js → "Introduced by Jess Newbould"
02 · Auto-fill

Companies House lookup

The Worker pulls the email domain, hits Companies House, and adds the matched company name, number and city to the lead row.

@jll.com → JLL Limited · 01188567 · London
03 · One-tap

Sign in with LinkedIn

OIDC popup. Returns name and verified email — fills the form for them. Falls back to manual entry on any device.

LinkedIn → name + email pre-filled · zero typing
04 · Compliant

GDPR by default

Privacy notice on the form. Lawful basis logged with each submission. Council privacy statement linked. Nothing dark-pattern.

Consent + clear notice · linked council policy

For the team
Where the leads land

A shared Excel sheet and a Teams chat. That's it.

No new tool to learn. The Worker forwards every submission into a Power Automate flow that writes a row to Excel Online and posts a card to the team chat. Auto-reply with the brochure goes back to the lead the same instant.
